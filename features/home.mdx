---
title: "Home screen — your music library at a quick glance"
description: "The home screen surfaces your recently played songs, favorite artists, and most-played tracks so you can start listening the moment you open the app."
---

The home screen is designed to get you to music immediately. Every time you open DiegoMusic, it shows you the songs and artists that matter most to you — no searching required. The screen is organized into three distinct sections that update automatically as you listen.

## Recently played

The top of the home screen shows up to **8 of your most recently played songs**, pulled from a list stored in local async storage. Each entry displays the song thumbnail, title, and channel name so you can recognize tracks at a glance. Tap any entry to start playing again right where you left off.

The list updates every time you finish or skip a song, keeping your most current listening history at the top.

## Favorite artists

Below your recent plays, DiegoMusic surfaces up to **3 of your saved favorite artists**, chosen randomly from your full favorites list each time the screen loads. This rotation is intentional: if you have more than three favorite artists, a random selection appears on each visit to keep the home feed feeling fresh.

Tap an artist card to open a channel overlay showing popular songs from that artist's YouTube channel. The same overlay appears when you tap an artist name in the player.

<Tip>
  To see an artist on your home screen, save them as a favorite while a song is playing. Open the expanded player, then tap the save-artist icon (the person with a plus symbol). The artist will appear in the **Favorite artists** section on your next visit to the home screen.
</Tip>

## Most played

The **Most played** section ranks up to your **top 10 songs** by how many times you have played them, sorted from highest to lowest play count. The ranking is tracked locally by a `timesPlayed` counter and updates in real time as you listen.

If you have not played enough songs yet to fill the list, the section displays an empty state message until your history grows.

## Stats overlay

The trophy icon in the upper-right corner of the header opens a **statistics overlay** that gives you a summary of your listening activity. The overlay shows:

- Your current **listening streak** — consecutive days on which you have played at least one song.
- **Play counts** for your most active sessions, so you can see how your habits change over time.

Tap the trophy icon to open the overlay, and tap anywhere outside it (or press the close button) to dismiss it.
