title: "Settings — configure your language and video quality"
description: "Change the app language between English, Spanish, and Japanese, and set the video streaming quality to balance clarity and data usage."
---

The Settings screen lets you configure two global preferences: the display language and the video quality used when playing music videos. Both settings take effect immediately — you do not need to restart the app. Open Settings by tapping the gear icon in the bottom tab navigation.

## Language

DiegoMusic supports three languages. Tap your preferred option to switch the entire app interface — all labels, button text, empty states, and error messages update instantly.

| Language | Display name |
|----------|-------------|
| English | English |
| Spanish | Español |
| Japanese | 日本語 |

The selected language is saved locally so your choice persists across app restarts. A blue checkmark appears next to the active language. The language preference is stored under the key `@app_language` in local storage.

## Video quality

The video quality setting controls the format selector passed to yt-dlp on the backend when you request a music video. Choose a quality level that balances visual clarity with your available bandwidth.

| Setting | Resolution | When to use |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| **Low** | 360p | Slow connections or when saving mobile data |
| **Medium** | 480p | A balance of quality and data usage |
| **High** | 1080p | Wi-Fi connections where data usage is not a concern |

The active quality level is highlighted with a blue checkmark and the corresponding signal-strength icon. Your selection is applied the next time you open a video — it does not restart any video that is already playing.

<Tip>
  Select **Low** or **Medium** when streaming videos over mobile data. A 360p stream uses significantly less data than 1080p and still plays smoothly on most connections.
</Tip>
