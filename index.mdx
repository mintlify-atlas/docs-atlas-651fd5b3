DiegoMusic is a free, open-source music streaming app for iOS and Android. It sources audio and video from YouTube using a self-hosted backend, giving you Spotify-like features — search, favorites, lock-screen controls, and offline playback — without any subscription fee.

## What DiegoMusic does

DiegoMusic is split into two parts: a **React Native mobile app** (built with Expo) and a **Node.js backend** that proxies YouTube content via yt-dlp. The app connects to your self-hosted backend to search for songs, stream audio, and play music videos — all without an account or a paid subscription.

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Deploy the backend">
    Run the Node.js server with Docker Compose. It handles YouTube scraping, audio extraction via yt-dlp, and video streaming.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure the app">
    Set `EXPO_PUBLIC_API_URL` in the mobile app to point at your backend server.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Install on your device">
    Build the Expo app for iOS or Android, or run it in the Expo Go client for development.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start listening">
    Search for any song or artist, tap to play, and save your favorites for offline use.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  DiegoMusic requires a self-hosted backend. See the [deployment guide](/deployment/backend) to get it running before installing the app.
</Note>
